About Me

I am a life-long learner. An early researcher in the intersection of medicine and engineering. Particularly, using Artificial Intelligence (AI) in healthcare to better care.

My career plan involves pursuing a postgraduate degree in Computational Biology (Bioinformatics) with interest in using biological data, such as genetic sequences, cell populations or patient-doctor conversations (speech, notes) to solve challenges in precision medicine and stratified healthcare.

I am building skills in statistics, programming, deep (machine) learning, biology, data stewardship & visualization and scientific communication.

My life's purpose is to positively impact my fellow human in the smallest ways possible.

Undergraduate Research Assistant

Centre for Data Science & Artificial Intelligence (DSAIL) | February 2022 - April 2022

Designed hardware and software for monitoring of knee rehabilitating patients using an Arduino microcontroller and python programming language with the mentorship of Professor Ciira.

The Arduino run on a C++ program which aided the measuring of knee flexion angles. While the two python programs, one connected to Arduino via Bluetooth Low Energy(BLE), stored data in a time series database (Influxdb) and the second python program, which run on dash, displayed the knee flexion angles stored in Influxdb on a real time dashboard.

This project offers a digital storage of patient data and cost-effective alternative for orthopedic rehabilitation, with the potential for remote patient monitoring and improved patient outcomes, with further development. More -> here.
