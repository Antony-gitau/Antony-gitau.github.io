About Me

I am a student researcher (intern) at Google Reseach Africa , where I am working on the food insecurity forecasting team with Dr. William Ogallo, Dr. Aisha Walcott-Bryant et al.

I just completed my requirements for an MSc in Mathematical Sciences (AI for Science) at the University of Cape Town (UCT) and the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS), South Africa sponsored by Google DeepMind Education Scholarship. (officially graduate in December 2025). For my MSc project, I worked on multimodal-RAG based pipeline for medical education assessments at Jamieson Lab , Texas, USA.

Previously, I was a Data Science Africa (DSA) Research Fellow, where I worked on computer vision approaches for monitoring recovery of orthopedic patients. I also contractually wrote technical articles for Zilliz to assist AI developers in using Milvus, a popular open-source vector database.

I hold a BSc. Biomedical Engineering from Kenyatta University, Nairobi, Kenya. During my undergraduate, I worked with Professor Ciira Maina at the Centre for Data Science & Artificial Intelligence (DSAIL), where I developed an orthopedic wearable device for recovery monitoring. I also visited Marconi Lab in Makerere University, Kampala, Uganda, where I worked with Dr. Andrew Katumba on developing Swahili speech recognition model, and with Dr. Jan Mikelson on breast cancer literature review for a hackathon participation at the University of Texas Southwestern. Both experiences resulted in peer-reviewed publications. I also interned at Talent Bridge Africa supporting clients with data-driven decision making insights.

I won multiple scholarships to participate in person in international data science events in countries like France, Norway, Greece etc. I also enjoy actively participating in communities such as KamiLimu, IEEE, Toastmasters, where I have organized events, lead new initiatives and mentored others.

Research Interest

My core research interest is in developing or improving existing machine learning methods. I am especially keeping myself updated in:

Foundation multimodal models.

- Fine-tuning LLMs, retrival augmenting, model concept protocol, multimodal data integration.

Probabilistic machine learning and generative models.

- Bayesian inference, causal inference, and probabilistic programming.

Privacy preserving machine learning methods.

- Enable training on decentralized, sensitive data without compromising confidentiality.

Reinforcement learning.

- With human feedback and AI feedback.
I believe in learning in public. So, I spend some time writing about the lessons I learn on my Medium page. You can also read my peer-reviewed publications on Google Scholar. I am also very open to collaborations, (virtual) coffee chats etc by writing to me at antonym dot gitau9 at gmail dot com.
